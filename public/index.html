Arthur Kauffman is a software engineer living in East Harlem. He is the co-founder of Campion, an open-source, edge deployable circuit-breaker middleware.

While teaching English classes at urban public high schools in Pittsburgh, Baltimore, and the Bronx, he relentlessly improved curriculum, founded and facilitated student-centered chess clubs, organized and chaired a teacher-mentorship program, and significantly increased student performance and after-school attendance.

As a software engineer, he brings his passion for collaboration and team-building into his work on open-source software. He believes in writing clean, human-centric code.

He occasionally writes about coding and other topics on his blog.
